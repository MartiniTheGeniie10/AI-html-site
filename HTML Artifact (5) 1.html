Handling AI access questions Clients are increasingly curious about AI. This section gives you a clear three-step approach for responding when they ask about AI capabilities, direct access to our systems, or enterprise-level tools like WPP Open Pro — so you always know what to say and when to escalate.

1

Acknowledge existing AI use

Make them aware that AI is already embedded in our campaign processes and Hero tools.

2

Be clear about boundaries

Direct access to AI through our systems is not possible.

3

Escalate when necessary

If they ask about enterprise-level software (e.g., WPP Open Pro), do not promise anything — ask an AI Super User for further information.

🌳

Navigating AI client conversations based on whether the client has raised the topic Not every client conversation about AI starts the same way. This section provides two ready-made playbooks depending on whether the client has brought up AI themselves or whether you're considering introducing it. Follow the steps to assess the opportunity, frame the conversation around business value, and know exactly when to involve your AI Super Users.

💡
Before any AI conversation, ask yourself: "Is there a real benefit for the client or our agency (e.g., better data quality, process automation, revenue)?"
Scenario A

Client has never mentioned AI or WPP Open

1

Assess the need

If no clear benefit exists, leave it alone.

2

Inspiration session

If there is a benefit, start with business problems, not tech. Offer a high-level "Awareness" session.

3

Escalate

Share a hyperlink to a previously run inspiration deck. For further information on inspiration decks, reach out to matt.cox@wppmedia.com

→

Next step

Contact your AI Super Users to arrange a workshop or define strategic alignment.

Scenario B

Client has mentioned AI or WPP Open

1

Address security

Walk them through "Data Fortress" principles — enterprise-grade security, segregated data, never trains public LLMs.

2

Find the business problem

Ask them to bring a real, messy, time-consuming challenge they face.

3

Pitch a pilot

Recommend a low-risk, high-visibility "Speedboat" pilot (e.g., 3-week test to prove ROI).

→

Next step

Contact your AI Super Users to use the AI Agent Development Canvas and scope the pilot.

What do I need to know when my client asks for WPP Open? When a client specifically asks about WPP Open, you need to be confident in your response. This section outlines the key boundaries — what clients cannot access, why, and what our strategic aim is — along with the alternative client-facing tools you can recommend instead, complete with contact details for the people who can help.

  • 🚫 The client cannot have direct access to WPP Open Media.
  • 🚫 WPP Media does not offer Open Media Studio as a standalone SaaS product.
  • 🚫 It is not our aim to create a dedicated Client Hub for each client.
✅
Our aim is to have all clients under the WPP Media Hub so internal teams can use Open for campaign and project management.
